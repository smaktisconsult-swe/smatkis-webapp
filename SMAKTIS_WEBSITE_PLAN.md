# SmaKTis Consultancy Website Plan

## Source Summary

This plan is based on the proposal document `SmakTis Business Plan and Proposal_Onireti.pdf`.

SmaKTis Consultancy is an educational support and life science research consultancy led by Jacob Olaleye Onireti, PhD. The business serves high schools, university students, researchers, academics, and biotech R&D/startups in Sweden, with a focus on high-quality, ethical academic and research support.

Core services from the proposal:

- Private tutoring and substitute teaching support in science subjects, especially chemistry, physics, mathematics, biochemistry, and molecular biology.
- Academic writing support, proofreading, project management, and teaching support.
- Wet lab consultation/assistantship, experiment planning, and data analysis.
- Scientific/manuscript writing, proposal preparation, literature reviews, and grant support.
- Biotech startup R&D support, molecular mechanism interpretation, biomolecular data interpretation, and drug signaling pathway/MoA support.
- Workshops, training sessions, and institutional support packages.

Business goals from the proposal:

- Year 1: 15-25 consistent customers per month and 30,000-40,000 SEK monthly revenue.
- Year 2: 30-40 customers per month through partnerships with research groups or biotech companies.
- Year 3: 100,000+ SEK monthly revenue with institutional partnerships and collaborators.

## Website Objectives

The website should do five jobs well:

1. Present SmaKTis as a credible Sweden-based academic and life science consultancy.
2. Separate services clearly for schools, students, researchers, and biotech/startups.
3. Convert visitors into consultation requests through clear calls to action and structured intake forms.
4. Build trust through credentials, ethical boundaries, process transparency, testimonials, and educational resources.
5. Support SEO for local Swedish searches and specialist research-support searches.

## Positioning

Recommended positioning statement:

> Academic and life science research support for students, schools, researchers, and biotech innovators in Sweden.

Supporting message:

> SmaKTis helps clients move from uncertainty to clear, well-structured academic and research outcomes through expert tutoring, scientific writing support, data interpretation, lab consultation, and tailored project guidance.

Tone:

- Professional, calm, precise, and trustworthy.
- More consultancy than tutoring marketplace.
- Ethical academic support, never ghostwriting or misconduct.
- Scientifically credible but readable for school and student audiences.

## Brand Direction

The PDF uses a formal academic style with dark blue and grey. The website can keep that trust signal while feeling more modern.

Recommended visual identity:

- Primary color: deep academic blue, close to the PDF header color.
- Secondary color: clean teal or molecular green for science and life-science accents.
- Neutral palette: white, cool grey, charcoal text, light blue-grey backgrounds.
- Typography: modern sans-serif for readability, with strong section headings and compact service cards.
- Visual style: real-feeling academic, laboratory, classroom, and data imagery; avoid generic corporate stock aesthetics.

Brand naming:

- Use `SmaKTis Consultancy` consistently in public copy.
- Use `SMAKTIS CONSULTANCY` only where a formal all-caps logo treatment is desired.
- Confirm whether the preferred spelling is `SmaKTis`, `SmakTis`, or `SMAKTIS` before final design.

## Video Plan

Video should be used lightly. The business is trust-based, so videos should clarify expertise and process rather than feel like advertising noise.

### Video 1: Homepage Hero Loop

Format:

- 8-12 second silent loop.
- WebM and MP4.
- Desktop only or reduced-motion alternative.

Concept:

- Close-up sequence of research and education objects: notebook, microscope slide, laptop charts, molecular model, and consultation notes.

Generation brief:

> Create a calm, realistic 10-second hero loop for a Sweden-based academic and life science consultancy. Slow camera movement across a desk with scientific charts on a laptop, molecular biology notes, microscope slide, chemistry equations, and a clean academic workspace. Deep blue and teal accents, natural daylight, no people, no logos, no readable text. Smooth professional motion, not flashy.

Use:

- Home hero background for desktop.
- Static generated hero image as fallback for mobile and reduced-motion users.

### Video 2: Service Explainer

Format:

- 45-60 seconds.
- Founder voiceover or text-led animation.

Script outline:

1. SmaKTis supports schools, students, researchers, and biotech teams.
2. Clients begin with a consultation to define scope, deadlines, ethics, and pricing.
3. Services include tutoring, scientific writing support, data interpretation, literature review, lab consultation, and workshops.
4. Delivery is structured, secure, and tailored.
5. CTA: book a consultation.

Visual approach:

- Mix generated visuals, simple animated icons, and real founder footage if available.
- Do not use fake client testimonials or fake institutional footage.

## Copywriting Requirements

Every page should include:

- Clear audience label.
- Plain-language service description.
- Outcome-focused benefits.
- Ethical support statement where academic work is involved.
- CTA near the top and bottom.

Avoid:

- Claims of guaranteed grades, publication acceptance, or grant success.
- Language that implies ghostwriting or doing assessed work for students.
- Fake partnerships with schools, universities, or biotech companies.
- Overly broad "we do everything" service wording.

- Static pages with a lightweight CMS later if blog/resources will grow.
- Responsive design for mobile, tablet, and desktop.
- Contact form connected to email and optional CRM/spreadsheet.
- Booking integration such as Calendly, Google Calendar appointment schedules, or a custom form-first flow.
- Analytics with privacy-conscious configuration.
- Image optimization and lazy loading.
- Reduced-motion fallback for hero video.

Core components:

- Header with Services dropdown.
- Hero section with CTA.
- Audience cards.
- Service cards.
- Process timeline.
- Testimonial/case study cards.
- Pricing/package summary.
- Contact form.
- Footer with contact details, service links, and policy links.

Accessibility:

- High contrast text.
- Keyboard-accessible navigation.
- Form labels and helpful error messages.
- Alt text for every image.
- Captions/transcripts for videos.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

Performance:

- Keep homepage under 2 MB initial load where possible.
- Use static image fallback for mobile instead of heavy video.
- Compress generated images to WebP/AVIF.
- Use short muted loop only when it adds value.
